The Department of Mechanical Engineering was established in 2004 with the objective of nurturing students for the ever-evolving field of mechanical systems and technologies. It offers a diverse array of programs including B.Tech. (Mechanical Engineering), M.Tech. (Mechanical Engineering), M.Tech. (Advanced Manufacturing), and Ph.D. (Mechanical Engineering). These programs are meticulously structured to equip students with the requisite engineering skills crucial for mechanical industries, coupled with a robust foundation in core subjects such as thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and solid mechanics. This emphasis on fundamentals facilitates curriculum adaptability, allowing for customization at advanced levels to meet the dynamic requirements of the mechanical industry. Students are empowered to select advanced electives tailored to their interests and the evolving needs of the mechanical engineering landscape.

What is Mechanical Engineering?

Mechanical Engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, construction, and operation of machines and mechanical systems. It applies principles of physics and materials science to create and maintain mechanical systems that serve various industries.

How is Mechanical Engineering applied in automotive design?

Mechanical Engineering is applied in automotive design to develop and optimize vehicle components and systems, such as engines, transmissions, suspension systems, and aerodynamics. It involves concepts like thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and materials science to improve vehicle performance, efficiency, and safety.

What are finite element analysis (FEA) and computational fluid dynamics (CFD) in Mechanical Engineering?

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) are computer simulation techniques used in Mechanical Engineering. FEA is used to analyze the structural behavior of components under various loading conditions, while CFD is used to analyze fluid flow and heat transfer phenomena. These tools aid in design optimization and performance prediction.

What are the ethical considerations in Mechanical Engineering?

Ethical considerations in Mechanical Engineering include issues related to product safety, environmental impact, and social responsibility. Engineers must ensure that their designs prioritize public safety, minimize environmental harm, and adhere to ethical standards in manufacturing and distribution. Additionally, ethical concerns may arise in issues such as intellectual property rights and conflicts of interest.
